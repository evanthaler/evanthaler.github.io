My name is Evan Thaler. I'm interested in how the Earth's surface (and near subsurface) evolves over time, which is a branch of science we call "geomorphology." We chose that name to sound super cool and confuse everyone else. Most of my reseach uses data collected from satellites and airplanes to look at patterns of the Earth's surface and try to understand what processes create those patterns. I am currently a Chick Keller Postdoctoral Fellow at the Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL). Prior to joining the lab, I received my PhD in Geosciences at the University of Massachusetts working with Isaac Larsen and Qian Yu and my MS and BS in Geology at the University of Arkansas working with Matt Covington.
